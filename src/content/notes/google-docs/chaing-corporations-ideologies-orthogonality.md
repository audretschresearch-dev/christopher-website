The heptapods' non-linear cognition creates a fundamentally different relationship to goals themselves. Their superintelligence doesn't optimize for outcomes in the traditional sense, as they already perceive all outcomes simultaneously. This suggests that at certain levels of intelligence, the entire framework of "goals" as we understand them might become inapplicable or transform into something unrecognizable.

Intelligence might develop values and purposes that emerge directly from their cognitive architecture, rather than being orthogonal to it.

New linguistic structures enable conceptions previously unthinkable, rendering conventional human objectives simplistic and arbitrary.

When intelligence can perceive and modify its own cognitive structures (as in "Understand"), this recursive self-improvement naturally leads to value reassessment. **The superintelligent entity recognizes that its previous value framework was limited by its previous cognitive limitations.
****
**Ideologies are essentially language systems that restructure cognition. Once you adopt the linguistic framework of any ideology, certain values become intelligible while others become literally unthinkable.

**Corporations, Ideologies, and Orthogonality
****Corporations
**This analysis suggests a fascinating relationship: corporations demonstrate relatively stable goal structures despite increasing specialized intelligence precisely because they lack the transformative aspects of superintelligence that would challenge orthogonality.
The most "intelligent" aspects of corporations (optimization, coordination, specialization) don't necessarily trigger value shifts. What's missing are precisely the elements highlighted in Chiang's fiction:
**Transcendent symbolic systems** that enable entirely new conceptual frameworks
**Radical perceptual transformations** that restructure how reality is experienced
**Autonomous meta-cognitive evolution** beyond human-designed parameters
**Ideologies
****Cognitive Architecture Determines Value Architecture**: Ideologies explicitly engineer frameworks for organizing perception and interpretation. Different ideological systems literally structure how adherents process information (seeing events through class dynamics versus individual choices), directly determining what values seem natural or important. This linkage between cognitive frameworks and values is central to ideological function.

Ideologies show that cognitive frameworks and value systems are linked in mutually reinforcing ways.
The more an intelligent system engages with fundamental frameworks of perception and interpretation (as ideologies do), the more likely intelligence and values become entangled.
Ideologies illustrate how even modestly enhanced collective intelligence can lead to value shifts when that intelligence focuses on restructuring perception and conceptual frameworks.
The most powerful ideologies explicitly recognize and exploit the connection between ways of thinking and ways of valuing—they are deliberately non-orthogonal.
Compared to corporations, ideologies more directly manipulate the symbolic and perceptual frameworks that shape values. While corporations primarily use intelligence instrumentally within existing frameworks, ideologies explicitly attempt to transform those frameworks.
__Systems that effectively manipulate perception and conceptual frameworks naturally violate orthogonality.
