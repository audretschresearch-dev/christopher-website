Boomerang Effect
If you present something under certain conditions, people will be more inclined to pick/believe the opposite of what is being presented.
Conditions:
When weak arguments are paired with a negative[?] source.
Argument is presented aggressively
When weak or unclear persuasion leads the recipient to believe that the communicator is propounding a different position to that which the communicator really intends.
When the persuasion triggers aggression or unalleviated emotional arousal.
When the communication adds to the recipient's knowledge of the norms and increases their conformity.
When non-conformity to their own group results in feelings of guilt or social punishment.
When the communicator's position is too far from the recipient's position and thus produces a "contrast" effect and thus enhances their original attitudes.

## **Tribe Creation
**
To create a strong tribe around a meme, appeal to group narcissism.
<Individual/Collective Narcissism Equivalencies>
Individualists on themselves:
1. You wish people would recognize your authority.
2. You have natural talent for influencing people.
3. If you ruled the world, it would be a much better place.
4. You are extraordinary.
5. You like to be the center of attention.
6. You will never be satisfied until you get all that you deserve.
7. You insist upon getting the respect that is due you.
8. You want to amount to something in the eyes of the world.
9. People never give you enough recognition for the things you've done.
Individualists on collectivists:
1. They wish others would recognize their group’s authority.
2. Their group has all predispositions to influence others.
3. If their group ruled the world, it would be a much better place.
4. Their group is extraordinary.
5. They like when their group is the center of attention.
6. They will never be satisfied until their group gets all that it deserves.
7. They insist upon their group getting the respect that is due it.
8. They want their group to amount to something in the eyes of the world.
9. Not many people seem to understand the full importance of their group.
