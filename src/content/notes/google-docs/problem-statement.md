So, the big question of our age is: how do we get a good outcome from superintelligent AI?
It seems like everyone thinks about this in terms of Alignment. I've been contemplating these things a lot, and I've come to think that Alignment is not the right way to think about this. (I can go into why I believe this.)
Noticing that Alignment might not be the right way to think about this is one step. There's another step, of course: what *is* the right way to think about this? What concept/paradigm/approach/lens *should* we use, then? Or an easier question: what alternative ways to think about <getting a good outcome from superintelligent AI> are there?
