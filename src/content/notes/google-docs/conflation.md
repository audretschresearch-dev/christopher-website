Many in AI safety seem to equate "the alignment problem" with "the problem of ensuring good outcomes from superintelligent AI." This conflates:
- the specific technical/philosophical challenge of getting AI systems to pursue intended objectives/values (alignment proper)
- the broader challenge of ensuring good outcomes from superintelligent AI systems
Treating these as equivalent assumes that solving alignment is both necessary and sufficient for achieving good outcomes. This assumption is nonobvious (and, I think, wrong).
