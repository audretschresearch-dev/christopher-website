**An Overview**
Here’s the basic idea:
There are objective normative (moral) facts. 
Normativity stems directly from the way reality is.
Normativity is non-arbitrary. 
Normativity is grounded in the phenomenology of valence (pleasure/pain).
Is and ought are related.
Sufficient understanding of reality allows one to see what’s good.
With sufficient understanding, the good *seems *like the good.
Sufficient understanding requires consciousness!
You cannot derive normative facts from third-person physical observation alone
We have epistemic access to (some) normative facts via consciousness and valence.
Normative stuff is infused into the “physical” world via consciousness and valence.
Normative facts – the objective moral truth – affects motivations and behavior in some minds.
Thus, normativity is entangled with the physical.
There may be “higher” forms of moral truth that we don’t have access to.
