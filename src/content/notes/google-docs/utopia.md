Utopia, Infinite ethics, we don’t know things
Jan 6

We don’t understand morality. We haven’t figured it out. We might even be asking the wrong questions. We might be using a bad framework for the whole thing.

Let’s talk about utopian literature. *What utopian literature? *There’s a lot of dystopian literature out there. Let’s broaden this to fiction, or even just “media”. There’s a lot of dystopian media out there. It’s easy to imagine dystopias. Utopias? Those are hard! A common form of dystopian media presents a world to the reader as a utopia; eventually either the reader “realizes” it’s actually a dystopia, or the author shows the “dark underbelly” that makes the supposed utopia dystopian.
So, where are all the utopias? Some people say we don’t have utopian media because utopias are boring. *Stories need conflict, and utopias have no conflict. So of course we read dystopian stories. *Or they say that utopian media is narratively or artistically uninteresting or bad for some other reason. Okay. I’ll grant this. But we still need to figure out what we want the future to look like. Perhaps as individuals most of us only need to consider what the future will look like at an individual scale. But leaders, visionaries, philosophers, political scientists – they need visions of utopia, right? 
I think I’m coming at this from an unnecessarily difficult angle. Let me try this.
We don’t know what utopia looks like. Various people over the years have proposed worlds that they claim are utopias. When we investigate them, though, we find that they aren’t actually utopias. 
I’m getting a little stuck here, so let me get to a different point.

**Non-dystopias
**Okay, different question. Easier question! Forget utopias: do there even exist non-dystopias? You might say: *look, I had an awesome three years in my late twenties. I had a bunch of close friends, we lived by each other, we hung out all the time, went on cool adventures, and at the same time I was developing well professionally, *and so on. That is, you can point to some existing (or historical) section of the world and say that this thing was basically good. 
So maybe my question seems stupid. But allow me to provide a very simple positive argument for it. If you look at any* *society in history, you find that it contains moral atrocities as well as a general level of daily suffering and unpleasantness for almost everyone. And this is true of our society as well!
