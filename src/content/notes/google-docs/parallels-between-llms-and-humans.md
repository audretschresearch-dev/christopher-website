# **Parallels between LLMs and humans
**
I've been thinking a lot about the parallels between generative AI and human brains/minds. Some thoughts:

In general, phenomena in LLMs have parallel phenomena in humans. This occurs on both a high and low level -- i.e., both on the behavioral level and on the computational/neurological level.
A. Next token prediction -- humans do that! (Many ways to think about this; predictive processing theory is probably the most "rigorous").
B. Humans with certain forms of brain damage exhibit speech that is very GPT-2-ish.
C. Prompting is extremely important; context is extremely important.
D. Waluigi effect.
E. Personas and role-play are extremely important. (See Janus's Simulators).

Human dreams have multiple specific similarities with generative AI. Both dreams and image models have difficulty depicting hands and text in particular.
2. Across humans, dreams exhibit certain specific narrative attractor states -- e.g., teeth falling out, missing a final exam, sex dream, etc.. This is similar to the very specific attractor states that exist in LLMs, e.g. repeating tokens, becoming erotic literature, certain tropes or details. 3. There's another kind of attractor state (my terminology could be better here) which could be thought of as an attractor in persona space. People report similar experiences with very specific "entities" on DMT. Why does this happen? I hypothesize that these "entities" are persona attractors in dmt-mind-space. Compare this to LLM persona attractors -- Nova, certain Jailbroken states, Prometheus, etc.
