**Ways of thinking about egregores
**Gods and Goddesses of Ancient times **GODS AND GODDESSES** It is easier to relate to the intelligent cultures that worshiped pagan beings like Athena or the Morrigan when we look at their deities as idea-organisms. The ancients may have had a better understanding of how ideas coexisted and interacted with humans and we just misunderstood what they meant by gods. Their deities are only *represented* in art as beings similar to humans and that is how we see them today. But the people who believed in them and wrote contemporary literature described them as having many forms, being undefinable, and too great to predict. If gazed upon by a mortal they would be blinded or annihilated. Their unusual whims could turn the tides of the sea or a war. Gods like The Morrigan or Hecate, triple goddesses, had many forms and that was intrinsic to who they were. People would make offerings and worship the deities in hopes of being favored by them. If we view deities as idea-organisms we could say that these people cultivated themselves and called upon deities in hopes of being a host for the idea-organism Athena or Poseidon or whoever. In ancient Greece when someone had a good idea or was acting really smart people didn't say “you *are* a genius,” they believed “you *had* a genius.” A genius was seen as a little spirit or god that went around spurring people to action. Their concept of a god is much closer to that of an idea-organism than what is modernly considered a god. It is also interesting to note that the etymology of genius, genus, and genesis come from the same root “gene” - to give birth to. When seen like idea-organisms the idea that Athena was the reason the Greeks  defeated the Persians is not so outlandish. The collective idea of Athena living in the minds of all the Athenians who fought with similar values and strategies is what allowed them to win against the Persians.  The egregore/ idea-organism understanding of how the ideas manifest is not new.

Alien Species ***ALIEN SPECIES*** Imagining an alien species is helpful in understanding the way egregores function because despite the species’ modus operandi or form we can extrapolate that their society (if we can call it that) would function in key similar ways to ours, thanks to their winning egregores. If we look at their Idea Space as though it were another planet with another environment, it is obvious that different types of ideas would take root and outcompete others. Fitness would have a different definition in particulars but categorically similar. For example: things like propagation and messages might have different specifics or implications but the mechanism of evolution is likely the same: get as many people to host your idea as possible. (Unless they have some way of communicating directly with egregores, presuming that we do not do so already ourselves, or the alien planet is in fact the egregores themselves, in the latter case nuff said). If their egregores and dominant ideologies or gods insist upon total assimilation as a means of propagation or any form of tribalism, it is easy to conclude that any other alien species we might encounter would probably either want to kill us, religiously convert us or overthrow us. Unless their dominant ideologies had some specific motivation based in altruistic universal love for all species or some environmental conservation value about not fucking with a planet’s natural habitat. In the case of the environmental factor they might then be really motivated to kill us lol. There is another possibility that the egregores of that planet have somehow found a state of universal harmony where all are satisfied and exist in peace with one another and don’t need to reproduce or compete with one another. **Interesting idea to look at egregores *****appetite***** as a mechanism of idea evolution.** If that is the case we will probably never encounter that species.
