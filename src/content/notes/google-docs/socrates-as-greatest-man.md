Socrates invented the western tradition of philosophy, which consists of basically two things: first, actually thinking about the big (“philisophical”) questions that can’t be determined empirically; second, approaching these questions via logical argument.

Importantly, the development of this way of thinking is not something that “would have happened anyways” without Socrates. Why? Consider other civilizations outside the Western tradition, such as China and the Native American civilizations. None of these had this approach to philosophy despite having e.g. mathematics and writing. Thus, the development of western philosophy is contingent upon Socrates.
