*If you joined Forethought tomorrow, what would you start working on, and why?
*
The broad question I’m interested in is: should we build ASI? The specific question I’m interested in, and the one I’d work on at Forethought, is: should we expect good outcomes from unaligned ASI via ASI aiming at the good? More specifically: under what metaethical theories would ASI aim at the good? More specifically, and the problem I’d start working on tomorrow: what relation do moral internalism and externalism have on whether ASI aims at the good? 

I think the question of whether we should build ASI is perhaps *the* most important question right now and one that I’m uniquely well equipped to think about. Building ASI is a pivotal act that will probably have extreme outcomes (and extreme moral outcomes). I think that most people are thinking about this question in the wrong way. They’re thinking about ASI outcomes in terms of the alignment problem: will we be able to make ASI do what we want? Theoretical work on this question, such as Yudkowsky’s, generally sticks to either a moral antirealist or philosophically vague view, and predictions about ASI behavior are tacitly made using this view. The problem with this way of thinking, and the key insight of my project, is that **the behavior of ASI may be governed by metaethical truths**. In particular, we can expect vastly different ASI outcomes depending on whether moral realism is true.

If my line of thinking is correct, then the interactions between metaethics, metaethical beliefs, and moral motivation are essential for understanding moral convergence in ASI. Questions that will be relevant are (and perhaps prepend “under what conditions” to these questions): Is moral realism knowable – can we know whether it’s true or false? Will ASI think about philosophy? Are moral beliefs motivating? Will ASI seek out moral facts? How will an ASI behave under metaethical uncertainty?
