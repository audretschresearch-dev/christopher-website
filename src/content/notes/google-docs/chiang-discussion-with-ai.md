Superintelligence isn't merely about processing information faster, but about fundamentally different ways of organizing thought.
Understand: ability to perceive and manipulate his own cognitive structures, creating increasingly sophisticated mental architecture
SoYL: experience time simultaneously rather than sequentially, perceives events teleologically rather than causally
SoYL: questions about free will and morality(?) are dissolved
Key point: different cognitive structure / conceptual framework dissolves paradoxes and unanswerable/impossible questions.
Hofstadter’s Mu: rejecting its framework rather than choosing between false dichotomies
What's complicated in one cognitive architecture may be simple in another

Cognitive architecture:
Thought is fundamentally shaped by its symbolic medium
Human cognition is constrained by the structures of human language
Certain concepts are literally unthinkable without the appropriate cognitive scaffolding
Superintelligence might require escaping these linguistic constraints
Chicken-and-egg problem:  to develop superintelligence, we may need new symbolic frameworks, but creating these frameworks might require superintelligence
Gestalts
We have this! Our deep insights seem to come in “eureka!” moments, sudden frame shifts, “everything suddenly came together” – and then we spend time translating the insight into words (and linear thinking).
Specific cognitive shifts:
Teleological vs causal
Simultaneous time vs sequential time
