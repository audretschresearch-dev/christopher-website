**Foundational Concepts
****Meme**: The basic unit of cultural transmission or imitation: an idea, piece of information, or behavior that spreads from person to person within a culture. Like genes in biology, memes are self-replicating units subject to variation and selection.
**Memetics**: The study of how information and ideas evolve and spread through human cultures, following principles similar to biological evolution.
**Memeplex**: A collection of mutually supportive memes that form a coherent belief system or cultural structure. Examples include religions, political ideologies, and scientific paradigms.
**Egregore**: An autonomous entity made up of and influencing the thoughts of a group of people. Originally an occult concept, now used in memetics to describe powerful, self-sustaining idea complexes that appear to have agency.
**Idea Space**: The conceptual environment where memes exist, interact, and compete with one another—analogous to an ecosystem in biology.
**Properties and Dynamics
****Memetic Fitness**: A measure of a meme's ability to survive and reproduce in the ideosphere. Determined by factors like memorability, spreadability, and durability.
**Memetic Properties**: Characteristics that contribute to a meme's fitness:
- *Memorable*: Easy to remember and understand
- *Spreadable*: Features that motivate people to share the meme
- *Durable*: Resistant to being forgotten or discarded
- *Aggressive*: Ability to compete with or suppress rival memes
**Memetic Evolution**: The process by which memes change over time through variation, selection, and retention, analogous to biological evolution.
**Memetic Mutation**: Changes to a meme as it is transmitted from host to host, creating variations that may be more or less successful.
**Advanced Concepts
****Memetic Engineering**: The deliberate creation and dissemination of memes to achieve specific outcomes—essentially, applied memetics.
**Memetic Immunity**: A person's or culture's resistance to certain types of memes, often developed through exposure to similar memes or counter-memes.
**Antimeme**: An idea with self-censoring properties that inhibit its own transmission, or that actively resist being noticed or remembered.
**Analysis Tools
****Memetic Analysis**: The systematic examination of memes to understand their structure, function, and propagation dynamics.
**Analysis Checklist**: A framework for evaluating memes that considers:
- Message (core content)
- Trigger (what prompts recall)
- Emotion (feelings evoked)
- Progenitor Mechanism (call to action)
- Social Currency (status benefits)
- Public Visibility
- Practical Value
- Narrative Structure
