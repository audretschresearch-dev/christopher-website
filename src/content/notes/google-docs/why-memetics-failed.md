__http://www.replicatedtypo.com/memetics-is-dead-but-whats-the-study-of-cultural-evolution-otherwise-about/10482.html__
*Summary: the piece is basically a post-mortem on memetics, explaining why it failed as a field. key points:
**- journal of memetics ran from 1997-2005 before dying from lack of quality submissions
**- bruce edmonds wrote a final "hey, this didn't work" paper
**- distinguishes between "broad" cultural evolution (still viable) vs "narrow" memetics (failed)
**- main critique: memetics was mostly just redescribing things in evolutionary terms without adding new insights
**- even former champions like dennett seem less excited about it now
**- the gene-meme analogy was too simplistic for complex cultural transmission
**- author suggests we need new foundational concepts beyond the "big things sending little things through tubes" model*
