This guy proposes a theory which is partly in opposition to memetics:

“I’d built my worldview around my own psychological and emotional issues. Once I saw this pattern in myself, I started seeing it everywhere.”
“I think that religious beliefs, political beliefs… are all part of our endogenous emotional opiate system, working together to regulate our emotional states within functional bounds.”

Memetics predicts that people have beliefs that are non-self-serving. Beliefs that push one’s emotional states outside of “functional bounds” in order to motivate one to spread that belief, even though spreading that belief isn’t self-serving.
