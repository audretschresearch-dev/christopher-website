Scott (pro-Ortho): The paperclip maximizer *does* understand that its goal is arbitrary. It can model human philosophers who would argue for different values. It understands that there's no objective reason why paperclips are the ultimate good. It just *doesn't care*.
This isn't a blind spot or selective stupidity - it's a coherent (if alien) value system. It's like how I can understand that there's no objective reason to prefer my children's welfare over strangers', but this understanding doesn't actually change my preferences. The is-ought gap doesn't disappear just because you're smart enough to see it.
