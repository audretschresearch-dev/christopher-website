I’ve spent the past few years developing independent research agendas in two areas: memetics (the study of cultural/ideological evolution) and ASI outcomes. My path here was perhaps unconventional. I majored in CS at Indiana University, but my primary passions were poker and philosophy. Poker led me to trading, and I spent summer 2019 interning at Jane Street. After graduating in Jan 2021, I spent three years as a proprietary trader at Tradelink, a firm in Chicago. My research was almost entirely independent – after initial on-boarding I had one meeting a year – and I developed a different approach to markets than the rest of my group. I made over $2M in net profit across 2022-2024, which was considered remarkable for a trader with my experience; in 2024, my profit was second highest in the fourteen person group. In February 2025 I quit and moved to Berkeley in order to focus on research questions I couldn’t stop thinking about.

The first topic I've been obsessed with is memetics, which I think is essential for understanding culture, ideologies, religion, and human belief. I've conducted an extensive "literature review" of memetics and have researched ideologies, cults, movements, and internet memes. I've developed a framework for memetic analysis that can be used to make predictions about the success of a meme/movement/ideology. 

But at Forethought I want to focus on the other, more pressing, obsession of mine – ASI outcomes: is it going to be bad or good if we build ASI? I've been fascinated with AI as a theoretical possibility since reading SlateStarCodex in 2018 and as an empirical phenomenon since GPT-3 came out in 2020. I fully bought Yudkowsky's doom argument for years, but in 2024 I started noticing what I think are serious flaws in the Orthogonality Thesis. This has completely changed my view on ASI outcomes - I now think superintelligence is more likely to be good than bad, though I'm quite uncertain about all of this. 

Right now I'm working on these ideas in relative isolation, which means I'm missing the feedback and collaboration that would actually help me figure out if they hold up. I’m applying to Forethought because I need to be around people who are thinking seriously about these questions, both to develop my own research and because I think I can contribute to others’ work. I love exploring ideas through conversation, and at Tradelink my colleagues often came to me to discuss their ideas and think through specific trades. I want to be somewhere I can work on what I think is most important. Forethought seems like exactly that place.
