Cialdini’s principles for human influence. [Examples via LLM prompts]

### 1. **Reciprocity**

- People feel obligated to return favors and kindnesses
- Even small gifts or gestures create a sense of indebtedness
- Example: Free samples in stores often lead to purchases
### 2. **Commitment and Consistency**

- Once people commit to something, they're more likely to follow through
- We strive to appear consistent with our previous actions
- Example: Getting someone to agree to a small request first makes them more likely to agree to larger requests later
### 3. **Social Proof**

- People look to others' behavior to guide their own actions
- We assume the actions of others reflect correct behavior
- Example: "Bestseller" lists, customer reviews, and testimonials
### 4. **Authority**

- People tend to obey authority figures and experts
- Credentials, uniforms, and titles increase compliance
- Example: Doctors in advertisements for health products
### 5. **Liking**

- We're more easily influenced by people we like
- Factors include similarity, compliments, cooperation, and attractiveness
- Example: Salespeople finding common ground with customers
### 6. **Scarcity**

- People value things more when they're rare or becoming less available
- Fear of missing out (FOMO) drives action
- Example: "Limited time offers" or "Only 3 left in stock"
### 7. **Unity** (added in 2016)

- People are influenced by shared identities and "us" versus "them" dynamics
- Goes beyond mere liking to shared belonging
- Example: "We're all in this together" messaging
