*Inspired by* [*The Company Man*](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JH6tJhYpnoCfFqAct/the-company-man) *and* [*How Much a Dollar Cost*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y8kEiL81_R4&list=RDy8kEiL81_R4&start_radio=1)

Imagine you're one of the obvious beneficiaries of modern capitalism: a tech worker, a 6 figure office worker, someone in finance or corporate law or such. Or you're big in entertainment: a big actor, a celebrity, an NBA player, a rapper.

(Me, I'm a trader.) 

You ask yourself: what's the price of this all, all this success?

[Kendrick Lamar:](https://genius.com/Kendrick-lamar-how-much-a-dollar-cost-lyrics)

> How much a dollar cost? 

How am I making 350k working for a no-name trading firm as a 26 year old with a 3.0 GPA from a state college? It's not like it's boring. In fact, it's the opposite. It's really interesting. It's intellectually demanding and satisfying. In some deep way it's *creative*. (Or at least that's how it feels to me). It's stressful, sure, but I like this particular kind of stress. There are no meetings, no deadlines. My work ethic kind of sucks to be honest; I often wondered when I was a teenager if I'd ever be able to do a "normal" job.

350k at 26. And the money only goes up, up, up from here.

My parents, my less technical friends, and I myself at times wonder: how can this be? Except we actually know how. It's the beauty of the modern economy. Finance and tech are high leverage, and there's simply a lot of wealth out there in the world. And I'm really good at what I do, and I have the exact combination of intelligence, interests, and temperament that's required for this particular job. I'm curious, genuinely deeply curious about markets. I'm rational, or at least [less irrational](https://www.lesswrong.com/) than most.. I'm really cool, really good under pressure, calm when the stakes get high.

> How much a dollar really cost?
> 
> The question is detrimental, paralyzin' my thoughts

But is my job actually providing any value? Market liquidity, market efficiency, blah blah blah. Okay, I could justify it.

And what of the entire global capitalist system this is built on? Okay, sweatshop workers in Bangladesh make my sneakers, I know. It's very sad. But it's the price we pay for the good stuff, and obviously the world is so much better than it used to be. I really mean it when I say there's no sense in getting hung up about this stuff, although we can acknowledge it's sad or whatever.

But anyways. I gotta focus. I can't sit here all day philosophizing. In the end, we gotta live life, and I gotta work.

> Parasites in my stomach keep me with a gut feeling, y'all 
> 
> Gotta see how I'm chillin' once I park this luxury car 

*Ding Ding Ding*.  Market closes at 15:00 CT. Time to go home.

Good day at work. I gotta walk from Wacker to Franklin to get my car from the garage. Just one and half blocks, and I enjoy the walk, especially in the summer. Do I need a car? No, but I love driving, and it is the fastest way to get around unless I wanted to Uber everywhere, which is aesthetically displeasing. And my time is worth a lot these days.

I said I enjoyed the walk, but some days I don't. You know it, I know it, it's the fucking homeless people. They suck. I'm getting radicalized on this. They destroy important social norms. They are legitimately parasites. I don't blame the individuals per se -- they're basically NPCs at this point -- but now that I'm paying a lot in taxes, I'm allowing myself to actually feel anger about it. Righteous indignation.

Get the car. Left on Lasalle, driving north now. I leave before rush hour, so the drive is actually quite fast. I love driving in Chicago, I'm suck a sicko, haha. Need gas, I'll stop at that triangle on Lasalle and Clark. 

I step out of my car and look at it. Admire it. There's no car culture here, so I'm not really flexing on anyone, but damn. I'm doing good for myself.

> Walked out the gas station 
> 
> A homeless man with a semi-tan complexion 
> 
> Asked me for ten Rand, stressin' about dry land

Worst part about gas stations is the homeless. And the worst kind of homeless are the crazy ones. The quiet ones don't bother me too much. The ones who ask you for money directly are annoying, but it's whatever. It's the crazy ranting type that really pisses me off. You just don't feel safe around it. Talk about poisoning the commons.

> Deep water, powder blue skies that crack open 
> 
> A piece of crack that he wanted, I knew he was smokin' 
> 
> He begged and pleaded 
> 
> Asked me to feed him twice, I didn't believe it 
> 
> Told him, "Beat it" 
> 
> Contributin' money just for his pipe—I couldn't see it

It's cringe to give money to homeless. *When he gives money to homeless on the first date -- total ick.* Come on, we know what's going on. Don't be so freaking naive. Nobody needs money for food, there are food banks everywhere, plus people give their leftovers to homeless all the time. You know he's gonna use that money to buy crack or fentanyl, right? 

Nah. Sorry man. Not happening.

> He said, "My son, temptation is one thing that I've defeated 
> 
> Listen to me, I want a single bill from you 
> 
> Nothin' less, nothin' more" 
> 
> I told him I ain't have it and closed my door

There's a friend of mine, a kind hearted Jewish guy who works at a bank, who actually gives homeless cash when they ask. I cringe every time it happens. People need to realize that when you give them money, you're incentivizing their behavior. You're telling the universe "I want to see more homeless people in downtown areas asking for money." It's a pathetic thing to do, actually, to let your in-the-moment empathy (or, let's face it, social awkwardness) cause you to do something that is ultimately *bad*.

It's *wrong* to give money to the homeless. Even if they aren't going to use the money to buy crack or fentanyl, which you can't know, you're providing perverse incentives. Actually, you'd be wronging *even that person you're giving the money to* because you're incentivizing them to continue to beg, rather than to clean up and get a job, which would ultimately be what's best for them.

Me, I'm well practiced in ignoring them at this point. I told him I ain't have it and closed my door.

> Tell me, how much a dollar cost?

A dollar is literally nothing to me. I could spare the dollar. I could obviously afford to give this guy a dollar, I could afford to give every homeless I see ten dollars. But it's wrong to give him the dollar. It's not about the money, it's about doing the right thing. 

> He's starin' at me in disbelief 
> 
> My temper is buildin', he's starin' at me, I grab my key 
> 
> He's starin' at me, I started the car, then I tried to leave 

I hate that they make me feel like an asshole for rejecting them. *They* make *me* feel like the bad guy. And I have to feel bad every time I walk past them. Why do they need to go to the most goddamn crowded places to do this? Fuck the homeless. 

> But somethin' told me to keep it in park until I could see
> 
> The reason why he was mad at a stranger 
> 
> Like I was supposed to save him 
> 
> Like I'm the reason he's homeless and askin' me for a favor

Once I make it to the safety of my locked car, I decide I'm gonna observe him for a bit. Is he crazy crazy, like, schizophrenic crazy? Or is he just acting in a way that's adaptive for his situation? Looking at me like I owe him something... that's the victim complex. Weaponized victim complex. I mean, it is adaptive. You can get a lot by using the victim identity. (Thanks for that, identity politics!)

> He's starin' at me, his eyes followed me with no laser 
> 
> He's starin' at me, I notice that his stare is contagious 
> 
> 'Cause now I'm starin' back at him, feelin' some type of disrespect 
> 
> If I could throw a bat at him, it'd be aimin' at his neck

Yeah, that's what he's doing. He's playing the victim card. You know what he's doing? He's dragging me into this. He's making his problems my problems. He makes bad decisions. He causes and is caused by a bad culture. And he's acting like it's my fault. The social justice movement is telling the world it's my fault. It's obviously not. You fuckers, want to know what's actually going on? People like me, people who have their shit together and actually try to do the right thing instead of just giving up and diving into fentanyl when things are hard, people like me are the reason we have a society at all. 

Sigh, but I *do* care, so I'm not gonna shift into first and hit the throttle and run him over right now like my emotions are telling me to. Like he's asking for. I'm going to be a good boy and sit here with my poker face. 

> I never understood someone beggin' for goods

You gotta love how the social justice movement, which exacerbated not only homeless itself but the problems downstream of it (crime, etc.) by getting rid of police officers, has the nerve to preach that homelessness is caused by  a lack of empathy, by the privileged people's *pride.* "Oh, you think you're responsible for your outrageous success in life? You, the educated cis white guy? Maybe if you realized that if it weren't for your privileged, you'd be on the streets too, then we wouldn't be in this situation. But you could never admit that, because then how else would you take all the credit for what the patriarchy has given you?"

> Tell me, how much a dollar cost?

Shut up, bitch.

> Guilt trippin' and feelin' resentment 
> 
> I never met a transient that demanded attention
> 
> They got me frustrated, indecisive and power trippin' 
> 
> Sour emotions got me lookin' at the universe different

I cannot fucking stand how they make me feel like I'm the bad guy. For working hard and paying my taxes and donating money to charity sometimes and keeping my shit together. You think I had it easy? I worked really hard, I powered through months of depression, I spent nine fucking months applying to jobs without getting one offer. That rejection felt awful. It hurt my soul.

> I should distance myself, I should keep it relentless 
> 
> My selfishness is what got me here, who the fuck I'm kiddin'?

I can't get sucked into this [empathy trap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Against_Empathy). I know where it goes. I need that [corporate psychopathy](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JH6tJhYpnoCfFqAct/the-company-man) . But it's not like it makes me a real psychopath. It's called being cool and making good decisions. 

I know how to succeed. It's by channeling my desire. If I don't want money, I'm not going to be a good trader. You have to want it.

And the beauty of modern capitalism, the beauty of free markets, is that we end up helping the world by acting in our own self interests. 

> So I'ma tell you like I told the last bum 
> 
> Crumbs and pennies, I need all of mines 

I'm worried I'm losing my desire. My wants are basically met. I have a great apartment, a great car, and enough money to not have to think about spending it. 

But if I don't want more, why would I still be working? How could I care about making money -- which is literally my job, my job is to make money -- if I don't want to make money? 

But making money is the *right thing to do*. We end up helping the world by acting in our own self interests. Making money makes the world better, in general.

North on Clark, get to my building, hand my keys to the valet, get upstairs into my apartment in front of my desk at 3:45. Futures markets close at 4:00. A last minute trade, closing out an ES position, a look at my daily PnL. $42k, not bad. Starting to feel hungry. Dinner at Nobu at 5:30. I'm making $350k a year as a 26 year old.

$350k a year as a 26 year old.

How much a dollar really cost?
