(A response to [https://sashachapin.substack.com/p/the-bay-area-is-cursed)](https://sashachapin.substack.com/p/the-bay-area-is-cursed))  
I've lived in Berkeley since Feb 2025. I remember reading Sasha's piece when it came out -- only three months ago! -- and feeling like I mostly agreed with it. And now, upon rereading it (sitting in an apartment in NYC on Jan 3, having just been to a good, high-eros NYE party here), I find that I mostly disagree with it.

What changed?  
First: I went to some events in SF in the past three months that were, simply put, more erotic. Two of these were burning-man-adjacent kinds of things. There were beautiful people there, and people put effort into how they looked, and there was a general eros in the atmosphere. And then on Halloween weekend there were another two events that don't fit Sasha's description of the Bay Area curse. Notably, these events were both fairly rationalist-y. On Halloween night, there was a house party at a literal EA house in Berkeley. I mean, don't get me wrong, this was by no means your slutty Halloween party from college or anything like what I imagine they're like in New York; but it was not erotically dead. On the night after Halloween night, I went to a party hosted by an AI-safety company in SF. [I wore a suit](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/a4tPMomzHhCunwqLX/you-can-just-wear-a-suit) and noticed some (not most) other people trying to look good. I didn't feel out of place.

Second: I've made friends with some hot people. (Here's my definition of hot: someone is hot if they’re trying to be hot. That is, someone is hot if they’re showing the world that they want to be seen as a sexual creature. Hotness, beauty, and attractiveness are distinct things.) Sasha says "The good, sweet men are scared of women. They find it unacceptable that someone, somewhere, could find out that they want sex." Not us! And I've become personally much more interested in hotness as an idea and practice(?), so I've been looking for it and seeking it out. Maybe an important difference is that in the Bay you have to seek it out, whereas in NYC it's everywhere?

Okay, so these are about how my experience has changed. But I also want to make a point that I don't really have the evidence for, which is that I think the bay area, including the rationalist scene, is genuinely changing too, and becoming less cursed in the way that Sasha is describing. In particular I think the part about the good, sweet men being scared of women has changed and is changing, but again, no evidence here.

Let me summarize. I used to think the Bay was cursed almost everywhere. That is, it was full of "dead" places, places in which people don't care about beauty and social life is "About something" and "bodily information doesn’t ripple from person to person, communication below the neck is limited". In the past few months I've experienced some non-dead places and made some high eros friends, and my current view of the Bay is that it has dead places and also non dead places. And I like that it has these dead places! I like that there are places where the ubiquitous power and obsession and distraction of beauty is gone! These aren't events you to go hang out with people in order to feel good, in order to get some of that nice natural human connection feeling, in order to feel eros, but that doesn't mean they're not valuable. And then when you do want that other stuff, you can find it elsewhere.
